Watchmen

"Watchmen" is a limited series graphic novel created by writer Alan Moore, artist Dave Gibbons, and colorist John Higgins. Published by DC Comics from 1986 to 1987, "Watchmen" is celebrated for its deconstruction of the superhero genre and its exploration of complex themes. The graphic novel's success led to its adaptation into a critically acclaimed film and discussions about TV series. "Watchmen" has left an enduring impact on the comic book landscape and popular culture.
